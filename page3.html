How to recycle

It is important when recycleing to sort trash into the appropriat bins. By properly sorting and prepareing your recycleing you ensure that your councils waste management will actually pick up your trash, and not leave it uncollected with a mean looking red tag attached.

Recycling Bins

Otago uses two diifernt bins for recycleing. These bins are used to recycle differnt kinds of waste. These bins are collected on alternating weeks, information regarding bin rotation can be found on your local councils website.

What not to put in blue bins

Not every peice of glass is able to be recycled in this little blue bin. Fragile glass such as cups cannot be recycled in blue bins as they would break during the collection proccess. This also means any broken glass cannot be recycled. Other enelgeble candidates are mirrors or sheet glass.

Preparing recycleables

Rinse your Bottles and Jars!

The friendly folk at the recycleing plant dont want to have to deal with you mouldy pasta jars. Do them a favor and rinse them out first. Rinse out any remaining contents from any jars or plastics you have.

Aluminium Cans and Tins

The lids of these cans cannot be recycled so they should be dicarded. It is also easier to recycle these cans if they are not crushed or bent.
